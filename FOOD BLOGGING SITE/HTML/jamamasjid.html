Top 10 Places To Try Street Food Near Jama Masjid

1.Aslam Chicken Corner

Everyone loves butter chicken, so one 18-year-old business capitalized on the trend and created a brand-new dish. The mint chutney with onions and Butter Tikka Chicken are the main things that keep customers coming back. Indeed, they offer Tikka, and the spices and tastes draw in the appropriate crowd. This restaurant is one of the best in Delhi for chicken.

Last updated 3 mins ago

2. Al-Jawahar

It was inaugurated by India’s first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, and has been named after him ever since. The kindness, as well as the cleanliness, is unmatched. Individuals visit this location more regularly because of the taste and aroma of spices that they put in the chicken. You may drool over the Al–Jawahar menu just by glancing at the variety of foods available. Al-Jawahar is also known as one of Delhi’s top restaurants.

Last updated 3 mins ago

3. Qureshi Kebabsa

Abdul Ghani Qureshi launched this restaurant, where you can have juicy and buttery meat kebabs for just Rs50, and the variety, as well as the more specialized products, changes. The flavor and originality of the Mutton Kebabs will wow you. This store specializes in crispy kebabs with onions, and the best way to eat them is without Rumali roti, simply biting into them and tasting the juiciness in your tongue.

Last updated 3 mins ago

4. Haji Mohd. Hussain fried chicken

The chicken is gently slitted and then marinated in a variety of seasonings. And then cooked in a frying skillet of oil, and sure, this restaurant still tastes the same after 40 years. So take a break from KFC and taste the chicken here; it’s delicious.

Last updated 3 mins ago

5. Cool Point

If you’re wary of chicken and want a creamier flavor on your tongue, stop by the ultimate sweet shop selling our favorite sweets. In a clay saucepan, they offer their spiced milk. As you get closer to the bottom, the milk becomes creamier, thicker, and more flavorful. It leaves a pleasant aftertaste in our tongue and is a must-try for everyone visiting Jama Masjid.

Last updated 3 mins ago

6. Nawab Qureshi Watermelon Shakes

Beat the heat this summer with a healthy and refreshing watermelon drink. The delicious pink tint will draw you in, and you may have a glass for just Rs10, and the stall’s name is Pyaar Mohabbat Mazaa. Yes, it is after sipping the drink. To keep the drink cold, there are also freshly cut watermelon slices and large blocks of ice, as well as Rooh Afza to provide color.

Last updated 3 mins ago

7. Ameer Sweet Shop

What is it like to visit a 100-year-old store, which conjures up images of pre-and post-independence America? With all of the chicken and mutton, as well as the biryanis, this is a wonderful location to eat. You may select from a variety of meals that are displayed right in front of you, making it simple to make your decision. Oh, and you’re free to try any of them.

Last updated 3 mins ago

8.Khalifa

This jalebi shop has been running since 1884 and is famous for its jalebis that are fried in desi ghee and then dipped in Khandsari sugar syrup which is made from the raw and unprocessed sugar.

Last updated 3 mins ago

9. Anwar Hotel

It is located across the street from the Karim Hotel, near Jama Masjid Gate No. 1. It’s only a little store that caters to those who enjoy spicy cuisine. It has seats for around 15-20 individuals, and the cuisine is reasonably priced. It also provides Paneer Tikka, making it suitable for vegetarians. Tandoori dishes with Rumali and Khamiri Roti are among the menu options. ice cream chain.

Last updated 3 mins ago

10. Shabrati

This hotel serves Mughlai cuisine and is located just across from Gate 1 of the Jama Masjid. It opens at 1 p.m. and stays open until midnight. It is a little pricey, but it offers adequate quantity. The service isn’t always great, but it’s worth checking out once in a while.

Last updated 3 mins ago
