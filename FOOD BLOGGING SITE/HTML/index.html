SOME OF THE BEST FOOD PLACES IN DELHI FOR FOODIES.

CHANDANI CHOWK

If you're a foodie and have ever been to Chandni Chowk, there's no chance that you would have missed Gole Hatti. The ever-popular joint whips up the craziest Chole Chawal with palak gravy which actually comes in a kulhad. They are spicy, super hot, flavourful and amazingly yummy!

CHANDANI CHOWK
JAMA MASJID

While talking about the main course meal, non-vegetarians should definitely try butter chicken boneless, Mutton Korma and Mutton Handi and vegetarians should try Kadhai paneer, yellow dal and more along with breads like khameeri Tandoori Roti. There are options galore; whether you are a non-vegetarian or a vegetarian.

JAMA MASJID
KAROL BAGH

The market is known for its shopping streets like the Ajmal Khan Road and the Gaffar Market (very popular for its electronic goods). The Karol Bagh market is visited by many people who come here to buy clothes, home accessories, home furnishing, food stuff and jewellery.

KAROL Bagh
KRISHNA NAGAR

Best Street Food Restaurant in Krishna Nagar · Bhola Shankar Kachodi Wale · Yashji Snacks Point · Delhi Dahi Bhalla Corner · Bhatia Sweets · Shree Ganesh Bhaturewala.

KRISHNA NAGAR
SHAHDARA

Sharma Bhature Wala, fondly known as “Afeem Wale Chole Bhature” is one of the oldest Chole Bhature shops in East Delhi. Don’t get skeptical about the word Afeem. It is called so because its taste intoxicates you. The shop is immensely popular for its delicious Chole Bhature.

SHAHDARA
Connaught Place

15 Best Street Food Places In Connaught Place · Anil Kachori Wale · Bhogal Chole Bhature · Odeon Bhel Puri Bhandar · Atulyam Jain Chawal Wale · Shukla Paan Palace.

Connaught Place
LAJPAT NAGAR

Treat Yourself With Delicious Street Food In The Lanes Of Lajpat Nagar · Momos & Chinese Chaat At Dolma Aunty · Ram Ladoo · Chole Bhature · Lassi.

LAJPAT NAGAR
KAMLA NAGAR

next to the North Campus of Delhi University, Kamla Nagar is a lively market and a popular hang-out for college students. It is famous for popular fashion brands and spicy street food. The roads, spreading out radially from a huge roundabout, house several showrooms.

KAMLA NAGAR

♦ Delhi Street Food Indian Street Food In Delhi Different people have different feelings about street food. Some people don't like eating out in open, mostly standing and surrounded by other folks. Others have misgivings about cleanliness and hygiene. Valid points but it's also true that flavors and innovation offered by street food vendors are hard to replicate in fancy, expensive restaurants. Street food is cheap and usually very delicious. Most vendors worth their salt have better standards than many branded food joints. You can see the food being prepared and cooking conditions with your own eyes. It’s unlike most established restaurants. In most cases, the cooking recipe is in the vendors family for generations and is zealously guarded just the way MNCs like Coca-Cola, KFC etc. guard their own recipes.
